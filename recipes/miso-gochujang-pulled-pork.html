Miso-Gojuchang Pulled Pork

Description

Asian-inflected take on barbecue pulled pork, inspired by the "pigalicious" wrap served at Bird & Ewein Sydney, Australia.

White miso and gochujang provide deep savoury-sweet notes and lots of complex flavour to oven-braised pork butt.

Miso usually is sold in the refrigerator case; gochujang, or Korean red pepper paste, does not require refrigeration until the container is opened. Both are available in well-stocked supermarkets and Asian grocery stores.

Ingredients

  • 5 lb boneless pork butt, trimmed and cut into 2 inch cubes
  • 3/4 c. gochujang, divided
  • 6 tbsp. white miso, divided
  • 1 bunch fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 c. hoisin sauce
  • 3 oz. fresh ginger, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 tbsp. grapeseed oil
  • 2 large yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • Kosher salt and ground black pepper
  • 3 tbsp. unseasoned rice vinegar
  • Pickled jalapenos, to serve

Steps

  1. Heat the oven to 325 °F
  2. Heat the oil until shimmering
  3. Transfer the pork to a large bowl
  4. Whisk remaining gochujang into the sauce
  5. Serve with cilantro leaves, pickled carrots and pickled jalapenos
